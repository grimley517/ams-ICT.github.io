Activity 1

Who's Who in computing

Learning aims:

  • Know who is who in the computing department - and who to seek for help, and who to report things to.

Head of Department

The head of ICT and computing is a focal point for all matters computing.

You should talk to the head of Computing and ICT if;

  • You are not sure who else to speak to.
  • You have technical questions that no-one else can help with.
  • You want to report damage to the computers.
  • You are trying to access a blocked website which you do not believe should be blocked.
  • You have accessed content which you believe is unsuitable, and should be blocked.
  • You have concerns about cyber safety

Your ICT Teacher

You should let your ICT Teacher know if:

  • You have a technical question.
  • You are trying to access a blocked website that you believe you should be able to access.
  • You have accessed content that you believe should not be accessible
  • You have concerns about cyber safety

Head of Key Stage and Heads of Year

You should speak quickly to a head of year or head of key stage if

  • You are being cyberbullied
  • You know of someone else who is, or may be, experiencing cyber bullying
  • You have any e-safety concerns

The Head of Mathematics

You should speak to the head of mathematics, ot your maths teacher about anything to do with 'mymaths'

Literacy Support

You should speak to a member of learning support about anything to do with Lexia.
